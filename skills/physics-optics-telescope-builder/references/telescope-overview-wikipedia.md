## What counts as a telescope

Historically an **optical** instrument using **lenses** and/or **mirrors** to observe distant objects. Today the word extends to instruments across the **electromagnetic spectrum** (radio, IR, UV, X-ray, gamma) and other detection modalities—**this skill focuses on optical (UV–visible–near-IR) design** unless the user explicitly widens scope.

Early **refractors** (Netherlands, early 17th century); **reflectors** followed (Newton’s practical reflector, 1668). Large professional instruments are predominantly **reflecting** because scaling refractors hits practical limits (~order **1 m** class mentioned in popular histories); modern monolithic primaries exceed **10 m** in reflector class.

## Optical telescope families

**Optical telescope** types (Wikipedia’s main trichotomy):

1. **Refracting** — lenses form the image ([refracting telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refracting_telescope)).
2. **Reflecting** — mirrors form the image ([reflecting telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflecting_telescope)); e.g. **Newtonian**, **Dobsonian** (mount + Newtonian culture).
3. **Catadioptric** — mirrors **and** lenses ([catadioptric telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catadioptric_telescope)).

Optical telescopes increase apparent **angular size** and **brightness** of distant objects by gathering and focusing radiation to a focal surface (eyepiece or sensor).

Specialized roles (astrographs, solar scopes, etc.) appear as **sub-types** in the ecosystem; treat them as constraint variants on the three families.

## Beyond visible light (orientation only)

- **Radio** — antenna / dish physics; interferometry for resolution.
- **IR** — sometimes mirror-like visible optics, sometimes sub-mm antenna metaphors depending on band.
- **UV** — often needs space or high atmosphere; **glancing** optics for EUV/X-ray ([Wolter telescopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolter_telescope)).
- **Gamma / high-energy** — often coded apertures or air-shower Cherenkov instruments rather than glass “telescopes.”

If the user’s task is **not** optical glass/mirror design, name the domain and avoid pretending this skill’s BOM-style advice carries over unchanged.

---

## Space vs ground

Atmosphere blocks many bands; **seeing**, **clouds**, and **light pollution** favor **space** for some science at cost of launch, size, and servicing. Ground remains strong where aperture and upgradeability win.
