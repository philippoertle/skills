# Vector templates for anchor and haul estimates

## Haul tension from MA

If a **lossless** machine supports load \(W\) with ideal \(\mathrm{MA}\), input force \(F_H \approx W/\mathrm{MA}\). With losses:

\[
F_H \approx \frac{W}{\mathrm{MA}_\text{eff}}
\]

State whether **\(W\)** is the victim+gear system weight or along-slope component on an inclined lip setup.

## Two strands into a master point (2D)

Let strand **i** have tension \(T_i\) and unit direction \(\hat{u}_i\) (from master toward rope run). Resultant on the anchor hardware (reaction on snow/rock) is often analyzed as:

\[
\mathbf{R} = -\sum_i T_i \hat{u}_i
\]

(Adjust sign convention to match your free-body choice—be consistent.)

## Equal tensions, symmetric geometry

Symmetric \(\pm\theta\) strands of common \(T\) give **along-bisector** resultant magnitude \(2T\cos\theta\) (derive once per problem; do not memorize without diagram).

## Angles matter

Small changes near **bad** geometries (e.g. near-180° turns, near-perpendicular mismatch to anchor axis) swing **components** strongly—call this out when the user gives angles.

## Order-of-magnitude checks

- Compare \(F_H\) to **one rescuer body weight** on a standing haul.
- Compare resultant to **typical** piece ranges only if the user provides rating context—otherwise stay qualitative.
