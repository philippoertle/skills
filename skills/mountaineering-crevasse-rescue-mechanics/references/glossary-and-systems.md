# Glossary and system notes (mechanics-first)

Terminology varies by school and region; **draw the rope path** to avoid ambiguity.

## Core terms

- **Mechanical advantage (MA)** — ideal from displacement or strand count; **effective** after friction (see rope-friction skill).
- **Progress capture** — one-way holding on a strand; analysis is often **phase-based** (captured vs advancing).
- **Lip** — rope bend over snow/ice edge; often the largest **capstan-like** loss and heat/wear concentration.
- **Minding** — managing slack, direction, and reset; not a substitute for force analysis but affects **shock** risk.

## Direct haul

Single primary strand team pulls; ideal force ratio order **1** on the load line absent mechanical purchase; **lip + biners** still change required haul force vs ideal.

## Z-pulley (pattern)

Common instructional **3:1**-class topology; real **effective** MA is **less** than ideal without quality pulleys and lip management. Analyze with: (1) ideal graph, (2) per-bend losses.

## Compound (piggyback)

One MA system advances a cord that is part of another MA; **ideal MA multiplies**, **friction compounds**. Useful to compare **theoretical** vs **effective** side by side.

## Drop-loop / quick 3:1 family

Names differ; identify by **which strand is fixed**, which moves with load, and where **capture** sits. Always derive MA from **constant rope length** if unsure.

## Anchor “physics only” reminders

- **Vector sum** at the master point determines **direction** of resultant on snow or rock.
- **Poor haul alignment** can **increase** lateral component on a protection piece even when MA is high.
